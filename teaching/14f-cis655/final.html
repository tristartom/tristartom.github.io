Final exam

Policy

The final exam will take place between 7:40 AM and 9:20 AM, Dec. 4th. The exam location is in LSB 001 [link] located in the basement level of Sci-Tech building.

The exam will be closed book, and only allows a cheat sheet (a A4 paper, double-sided) and a calculator-only device; any other electronic devices including electronic watches, tablet, etc, will be prohibited. The final exam should be done individually. No electronic device (other than calculator) will be allowed, and close monitoring will be enforced during the exam. Cheating behavior will NOT be tolerated.

Protocol

Prior to the exam

Seats in the final exam will be pre-assigned in a random and ad-hoc fashion. On the morning of Dec. 4 th, students may arrive around 15 min before the exam starts (i.e. 7:25 AM), and must sit where our TAs ask you to.

During the exam

Turn off your mobile phone, and put your SUID card on your table. Only cheat sheet is allowed; the final exam will have sufficient blank papers and you are not allowed to bring your own blank paper.

End of the exam

When time is up (i.e. on 9:20 AM Dec. 4th), you must stop writing and stand up to wait for TAs to collect their exam. You have to turn in all 20 pages (including the blank pages). If you are caught writing after 9:20 AM, you will get penalty of point deduction.

Scope

The final exam will be primarily based on sample exam, slides, and homework (use textbook as reference and secondary material). The sample exam can be found in Blackboard under class sessions->final (If you can not find the sample exam, contact our grader Venkata Harsha Sabbathi [email] for a physical copy).

The final exam will cover the following topics with emphasis on the bold text. You may want to pay extra attention to the examples in the slides and sample exam.

  1. Introduction:
    • Measurement: dependability, CPI, speedup, Amdahl's law
  2. CPU and ILP
    • Pipelining, dependency and hazard
    • SW ILP: compiler scheduling(slides-2.2), loop unrolling(2.2), register renaming
    • HW ILP: branch prediction(2.3), out-of-order execution, Tomasulo's algorithm(2.4)
  3. Memory and cache
    • Memory: virtual memory, page table(3.3), TLB, memory protection, virtualization
    • Cache: associativity(3.1), LRU, write policy, cache optimizations, cache and page table(3.1)
  4. Multiprocessing and TLP
    • Taxonomy: SMP, MPI, DSM, Many-core, SMT
    • Process synchronization: consistency, atomic instruction, locking/mutex, barrier
    • Cache coherence: MSI/EMSI (4.1,4.2), false sharing, directory-based coherence
    • Memory consistency
  5. Interconnect and storage
    • Interconnect: classification, routing, topology, deadlock, network interface
    • Storage: Medium, reliability, RAID(5.3), log-structured design
    • IO and bus
  6. WSC and RLP
    • Platform: rack, desktop-class, performance, power supply
    • Infrastructure: cluster scheduling, maintenance and tracing, programming frameworks, storage services, WSC networking, energy efficiency
    • Application: workloads, SLA
